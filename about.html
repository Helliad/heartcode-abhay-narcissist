Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD) Information

Learn about the symptoms, how narcissists are oftenly described as, causes and complications.

Let's Learn!

Symptoms

  • Self-centered
  • Lack of empathy
  • Exaggerate achievements and talents
  • Have an inability or unwillingness to recognize the needs and feelings of others
  • Be envious of others and believe others envy them
  • Monopolize conversations and belittle or look down on people they perceive as inferior

Often described as

  • Cocky
  • Manipulative
  • Selfish
  • Patronizing
  • Demanding

Causes

  • Childhood abuse or neglect
  • Excessive parental pampering
  • Unrealistic expectations from parents
  • Genetics ― inherited characteristics
  • Neurobiology — the connection between the brain and behavior and thinking

NPD Complications

A narcissistic personality disorder causes problems in many areas of life, such as relationships, work, school or financial affairs. People with narcissistic personality disorder may be generally unhappy and disappointed when they're not given the special favors or admiration they believe they deserve. They may find their relationships unfulfilling, and others may not enjoy being around them.

  • Become impatient or angry when they don't receive special treatment
  • Have significant interpersonal problems and easily feel slighted
  • React with rage or contempt and try to belittle the other person to make themselves appear superior
  • Have difficulty regulating emotions and behavior
  • Experience major problems dealing with stress and adapting to change
  • Feel depressed and moody because they fall short of perfection
  • Have secret feelings of insecurity, shame, vulnerability and humiliation

People with narcissistic personality disorder may not want to think that anything could be wrong, so they may be unlikely to seek treatment. If they do seek treatment, it's more likely to be for symptoms of depression, drug or alcohol use, or another mental health problem.
